About

RSD-FM @ AAAI 2026

Welcome to the AAAI'26 Workshop on Shaping Responsible Synthetic Data in the Era of Foundation Models (RSD‑FM).

Foundation models (LLMs and multimodal FMs) are increasingly supplemented with synthetic and LLM-generated data - to extend training corpora, fill coverage gaps, and navigate privacy/fairness expectations. At the same time, these models serve as powerful generators of synthetic data for downstream applications (e.g., training specialized ML models, augmenting datasets in privacy-sensitive domains, generating test cases for system validation).

But synthetic datasets, whether used to train FMs or generated by them for other uses, introduce legal (copyright, consent), security & privacy (leakage, membership inference), and technical risks (bias amplification, model collapse, quality degradation).

This workshop examines how synthetic data can be responsibly generated and used to fuel, test, and govern foundation models across their lifecycle (pre-training, fine-tuning, evaluation, auditing), as well as how FM-generated synthetic data impacts downstream applications and systems, and what technical, ethical, and regulatory guardrails are needed across this synthetic data ecosystem.

Topics of Interest (But not limited to)

Lifecycle Uses & LLM‑Driven Generation

Synthetic data for pre‑training, fine‑tuning (RLHF/RLAIF), continual eval; self‑training/bootstrapping loops and their limits.

Privacy, Security & Data Governance

Differential privacy and other PETs; leakage/membership‑inference risks; consent, copyright, provenance; comparative regulatory views.

Fairness, Bias & Representation

Measuring/mitigating bias via synthetic augmentation; balancing utility, privacy, and group fairness; surfacing underserved groups and edge cases.

Safety, Robustness & Red‑Teaming

Synthetic adversarial probes, jailbreak tests, and edge‑case simulations; preventing collapse/shortcut learning via healthy real/synthetic mixes.

Explainability, Interpretability & Uncertainty

Synthetic counterfactuals/narratives for debugging; uncertainty quantification; cross‑domain benchmarks (table, timeseries, text, vision, multimodal).

Standards, Metrics & Tooling for Trustworthy Use

Fidelity/utility/privacy metric suites; responsible generation protocols (source curation, prompt filtering, DP noise, provenance/watermarking, disclosure "data cards"); validation pipelines and audit checklists; open vs. commercial generators.

Submission Instructions

Submissions are processed in OpenReview. Submissions should be double-blind, no more than 4 pages (excluding references). An optional appendix of any length can be put at the end of the draft (after references).

Our workshop does not have formal proceedings, i.e., it is non-archival. Accepted papers and their reviews will be made public (after the review process), while rejected and withdrawn papers and their reviews will remain private.

We welcome submissions from novel research, ongoing (incomplete) projects, draft currently under review at other venues. In particular, we have the following policies.

  • [Submission on previous conference and workshop papers] We request significant updates if the work has been presented at conferences or workshops before, or has been presented at any conferences or workshops before.
  • [Submission on previous journal papers] For published work in journals that have not been presented at conferences or workshops, we welcome such submissions as long as it is novel it is for the community.
  • [Dual submission to other workshops at the same time] We generally allow dual submissions to other workshops at the same time.
